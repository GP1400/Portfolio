Wind Farm Suitability Analysis for West Virginia

GIS-based multi-criteria decision analysis to identify optimal locations for utility-scale wind energy development in West Virginia.

Project Overview

West Virginia's wind power potential is on par with that of the Rocky Mountains and the Midwest. This project explores this concept to pinpoint the specific areas where wind power generation would be the most suitable based on natural constraints, human acceptability, and economic viability.

Tools & Data

This analysis integrates environmental, social, and economic spatial datasets to represent real-world siting constraints.

ArcGIS Pro
  • Spatial Analyst
  • Raster Calculator
  • GIS Raster and Vector Analysis
  • Multi-criteria Decision-making (MCDA)
  • Raster reclassification & normalization
Core datasets used
  • Wind speed datasets (NREl WIND Toolkit)
  • Elevation and slope (LiDAR-derived DEMS
  • Roads, railroads, transmission lines
  • Urban areas (NAIP LULC 2016)
  • Airports
  • Parks, protected lands, and wildlife areas
  • Water bodies

Methodology

This project uses a mutliple criteria decision-making (MCDA) framework with equal weighting, avoiding expert-bias-driven weighting schemes such as Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP).

Environmental Exclusions

Protected lands, water bodies, wildlife habitat, and airport safety buffers were fully excluded from development.

Human Acceptability

Suitability increases with distance from urban areas to minimize visual and noise impacts.

Economic Viability

Wind speed, slope, and proximity to infrastructure determine construction feasibility.
