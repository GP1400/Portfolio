Wind Farm Suitability Analysis for West Virginia

GIS-based multi-criteria decision analysis to identify optimal locations for utility-scale wind energy development in West Virginia.

Project Overview

West Virginia generates roughly 95% of its electricity from coal, yet portions of the state possess strong potential for utility-scale wind energy. This project evaluates where wind farms could be developed while balancing environmental protection, human acceptability, and economic feasibility. Using GIS-based multi-criteria decision analysis (MCDA), I modeled statewide wind farm suitability to identify regions that warrant further site-specific investigation.

Methodology

This project uses a mutliple criteria decision-making (MCDA) framework with equal weighting, avoiding expert-bias-driven weighting schemes such as Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP).

Suitability Criteria

Pedestrian networks were constructed using walkable infrastructure, with impedance values adjusted for slope derived from LiDAR data. Evacuation routes were generated from populated origin points to designated safe zones outside tsunami inundation areas.
