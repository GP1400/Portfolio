Food Retailers of WV

"There's more to a food desert than having no food at all. It's the accessibility to food and the accessibility to decent food." — Nettie Freshour

Project Overview

What is the problem?

The reason this dashboard was created was to be able to visualize the different food retailers and be able to start a conversation, at the very least, about food access throughout the state of West Virginia.

Why does it need to be solved?

Food access is not evenly distributed throughout the community, and not all food retailers are created equally. Some retailers, while listed as being a food retailer, don't accept WIC, nor do they have fresh food. The limitation of fresh food is one of the biggest problems when trying to improve the health of the community.

Methodology

The problem was solved through the creation of this dashboard and just playing around with the map, seeing what different types of food retailers are available throughout the state and counties.

Results

The dashboard was created as a way to showcase food stores in the state of West Virginia. There are 4 main sections in the dashboard.

List Area

  • An ever-changing list of the food stores being shown on the map field, which also has a search feature for a specific store.
  • The list section also includes a list of the number of food stores in each county; this does not change based on the map.

Map Area

  • An interactive map that shows the outline of the state and counties of West Virginia. There are indicators for every food store in West Virginia with different colors, allowing for a distinction between the different types.
  • Selecting one food retailer allows for more information to be viewed, such as address, whether they accept WIC, and whether they have fresh produce.

Bar Chart

The bar chart is reactive to the map view and shows the number of different types of food stores shown in the map display. Selecting one of the types also allows for filtering on the map display of only that type of food store.

Pie Charts

The first pie chart shows the percentages of food stores that have fresh food, and the other does the same for WIC acceptance. Selecting one of the pie chart slices filters the map to display only that type.

Limitations and Conclusions

Limitations

   One of the big limitations of this dashboard is its usability of the dashboard for a mobile display. The amount of information makes a mobile display not really functional for all aspects of the information provided.
   Another limitation is the accuracy of the information for the food retailers. Some food retailers might not be included in the list even if they do provide some food, even fresh food, like gas stations that have fresh fruits or even fruit stands, that, while not traditional food retailers, do provide access.

Conclusions

Understanding food access throughout the state of West Virginia can be done through the use of this dashboard by understanding not only the types of food retailers, but also the type of access to WIC and fresh food each retailer provides. The hope would be to be able to use this information to understand where incentives could be placed for access to fresh food and WIC retailers to be a better distribution throughout the state as a whole, or even just the county.
