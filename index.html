About Me

J"Progress Not Perfection"

Growing up, I always loved maps and trying to figure out how things worked and why things worked by doing. This curiosity about things and life helped me excel in school and allowed me to graduate with an Associate of Arts at the same time I graduated from High School. This experience allowed me to find out early on what I loved about geography.

I attended the University of Wisconsin-Madison and even studied abroad in New Zealand at Massey University for a semester. I was able to focus my studies to get a dual degree in Geography and Environmental Studies, while also taking a few classes in transportation planning and transportation engineering.

Since graduating, I have grown a lot personally and am now in the midst of graduating with my Master’s of Science from West Virginia University in Geographic Information Systems and Spatial Analysis. This has been a good refresher in the work of GIS, and I am ready to put my all into the GIS world.
