About Me

"Progress Not Perfection"

   Growing up, I loved maps and was driven by a desire to understand how and why things work. This curiosity helped me excel academically and earn an Associate of Arts degree alongside my high school diploma.
   I continued my education at the University of Wisconsin–Madison, initially in civil engineering, but quickly discovered that Geography was where my true passion lived and made the switch early on. While still in engineering, I had the opportunity to lay the foundation for my love of transportation by taking coursework in transportation engineering and transportation planning. Geography being my true calling, I went on to earn a dual degree in Geography and Environmental Studies. I was also fortunate to study abroad at Massey University in New Zealand, where I further broadened my perspective through classes in urban planning and emergency management.
   I have since grown significantly both personally and professionally, and I am now completing my Master of Science in Geographic Information Systems and Spatial Analysis at West Virginia University. This graduate program has deepened my technical expertise and reinforced my commitment to applying GIS to real-world challenges.
